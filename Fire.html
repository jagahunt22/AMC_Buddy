FIRE

According to Andhra Pradesh Fire Service Act, 1999, Residential Buildings of height more than 18 mts ( high rise buildings ), Commercial buildings of height 15 mts and above and buildings of public congregation like Educational Buildings, Cinema Theatres, Function Halls and other Assembly Buildings on plot area of 500 sqmt and above or of height above 6 mt are required to obtain prior clearance from Andhra Pradesh State Disaster Response & Fire Services Department from fire safety point of view.


Types of Fire NOC

Provisional Fire NOC : The applicant should apply for the proposed building i.e on open land (before Construction) in the prearranged form along with prescribed fees both refundable and non-refundable to the issuing authority. The plans shall be clearly marked and attested to indicate fire protection arrangements and means of escape with standard signs and symbols. If any deficiencies are found they must be rectified.


Occupancy Fire NOC : The applicant should apply after completion of the building and fire fighting installation as approved by the building sanctioning authority to the concerned issuing authority with prescribed fees. Any deficiencies found in provisional NOC need to be rectified at this stage.


Renewal of Fire NOC : On annual basis Assembly buildings, Business and Mercantile buildings of height above 15 mts, Educational buildings and Institutional buildings need to be renew and obtain the latest Fire Clearance.


NOTE

Application can be submitted either by online or offline mode usually depends on relaxation letter if any.
If any area of land is surrendered to the Government, then a relaxation letter has to be obtained from the concerned authority and submit to fire officials. Issuing authority depends on the height and type of the structure

FAQ FOR SEEKING NOC FOR FIRE CLEARANCE

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) provides easy answers to the applicant queries, seeking Fire clearance.

Q.1. What is Fire NOC all about? keyboard_arrow_down

Ans. Fire NOC issued by the respective state fire service verifies that a building is resistant or unlikely to observe any fire related accidents. By meeting certain guidelines laid down by the fire department, an applicant can obtain NOC for his residential/ commercial building. The guidelines vary according to different states.

Q.2. Why is Fire NOC important for your building? keyboard_arrow_down

Ans. Statistics indicate that every year, thousands of people are killed across the world as a consequence of fire and fire related accidents. Moreover property and goods worth crores of rupees are destroyed every year due to fire outbreak.
Therefore it is important to secure Fire NOC to ensure safety for yourself and your business.

Q.3. Which buildings are required to obtain Fire NOC? keyboard_arrow_down

Ans. High-rise or multistorey buildings which are more than 15 meters in height are required to obtain Fire NOC from the state Fire Services.

Q.4. What is the process to apply for Fire NOC? keyboard_arrow_down

Ans. The application for obtaining no objection certificate may be submitted by the builder/owner of the premises to the Authority. The owners/builders are further advised to ensure the compliance of all the fire safety guidelines before for No Objection Certificate..

Q.5. Who grants the NOC for the building? keyboard_arrow_down

Ans. Fire Service of the respective state grants NOC to the builders. In case of AP, AP Fire Services is the concerned authority.

Q.6. Who heads the fire prevention wing? keyboard_arrow_down

Ans. The fire prevention wing is headed by a Director General and assisted by a Director, RFO, DFO and Station Officers.

Q.7. How much time is required for obtaining Fire NOC? keyboard_arrow_down

Ans. The fire safety guidelines are normally issued within four weeks from the date of receipt of request if all required information is correctly provided.
